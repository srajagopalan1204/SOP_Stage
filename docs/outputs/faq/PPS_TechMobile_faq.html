PPS – Tech Mobile – Frequently Asked Questions

This FAQ supports the M1. Tech Mobile SOP. It explains how technicians use Tech Mobile to move through a trouble-service call from appointment scheduling, travel, and on-site work, through additional material / work decisions, travel leaving site, and finally closing or marking the call as callback.

Questions & Answers

1. What is the purpose of the Tech Mobile SOP?

The Tech Mobile SOP describes the end-to-end process a field technician follows on a trouble service call using Tech Mobile. It covers:

  • S1. Appointment is Scheduled for Trouble Service
  • S2. Travel to the Appointment / Site
  • S3. Perform On-Site Work
  • S4. Add Additional Material
  • S5. Create New SRO
  • S6. Travel Leaving Site
  • S7. Change Status to indicate Call Back service
  • S8. Change Status to Closed
  • End / sub-SOP markers such as S998 / S999.

The goal is consistent, visible handling of every service visit from start to finish.

2. Where does the Tech Mobile SOP start and end?

The SOP starts when an appointment is scheduled for trouble service (S1) and the call appears for the technician in Tech Mobile.

It ends after the technician has:

  • Travelled to the site (S2).
  • Performed the required on-site work (S3).
  • Handled any additional material or additional work decisions (Q1, Q2, Q3 with S4 and S5).
  • Recorded travel away from the site (S6).
  • Either closed the job (S8) or marked it for callback (S7), and/or transitioned via an end/sub-SOP step (S998 / S999).

After that, the job can move on to billing or follow-up scheduling.

3. How does appointment scheduling (S1) connect to Tech Mobile?

S1. Appointment is Scheduled for Trouble Service is the trigger for the Tech Mobile process:

  • A service call / SRO is created and scheduled in the back-office system.
  • The technician sees the assigned job in Tech Mobile.
  • When ready to go, the technician updates status to travel (S2) and heads to site.

This ensures the technician, dispatcher, and office are all working from the same job record.

4. What does “Travel to the Appointment / Site” (S2) represent?

S2. Travel to the Appointment / Site is used when the technician is leaving their starting point and heading toward the customer location:

  • Status is updated in Tech Mobile to show they are en route.
  • Dispatch can see that the technician is travelling.
  • Travel time can be captured according to local rules.

When the technician arrives at the site, they move into S3. Perform On-Site Work.

5. How are additional materials handled (Q1 and S4)?

When the technician discovers that extra parts are needed beyond what the original SRO shows:

  • They answer Q1. Additional Material Needed?
  • If the answer is Yes, they follow S4. Add Additional Material to record the extra part usage.

This helps keep material usage, inventory, and billing aligned with the real work done on-site.

6. When is a new SRO created for extra work (Q2 and S5)?

If the technician identifies work that is clearly beyond the scope of the original SRO:

  • They answer Q2. Additional Work Required Other Than That Shown in Original SRO?
  • If the answer is Yes, they use S5. Create New SRO to create a separate SRO for that additional work.

This keeps the original SRO focused on the original request and gives the extra work its own document, pricing, and billing path.

7. How does “Can the Additional Work Be Completed At That Time?” (Q3) affect the flow?

After deciding that additional work is required, the technician must decide whether it can be completed during the current visit:

  • Q3. Can The Additional Work Be Completed At That Time?
  • If Yes, the technician completes the extra work in the same visit, then moves on to Travel Leaving Site (S6) and eventually Close (S8).
  • If No, the technician uses S7. Change Status to indicate Call Back service so a return visit can be scheduled.

This separates same-visit completion from true callback work.

8. What is the purpose of “Travel Leaving Site” (S6)?

S6. Travel Leaving Site is used when the technician is leaving the customer site:

  • Status is updated to show the technician is travelling away from the site.
  • Travel time back to shop or to the next job can be tracked.
  • The office can see that on-site work is complete.

After S6, the technician or office moves the SRO toward callback (S7) or closure (S8) as appropriate.

9. When should I use “Call Back” (S7) versus “Closed” (S8)?

Use S7. Change Status to indicate Call Back service when:

  • Additional work must be done on a later visit.
  • The customer’s issue is not fully resolved yet.

Use S8. Change Status to Closed when:

  • All planned work on that SRO is complete.
  • Any additional material / work has been recorded.
  • The job is ready for billing and closure.

Correctly distinguishing between S7 and S8 improves schedule accuracy and customer service.

10. What do S998 and S999 represent in the Tech Mobile SOP?

The Tech Mobile SOP uses S998 and S999 as end / routing markers:

  • S998 is often used as a restart or selection step that routes the flow.
  • S999. via S998. END indicates the end of this SOP or a handoff into a sub or separate SOP that continues the process.

These markers make it clear where Tech Mobile finishes and where another SOP takes over, while keeping the overall service process traceable end-to-end.
