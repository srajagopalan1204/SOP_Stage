PPS – Service Rental – Generator Rental Process – Frequently Asked Questions

This FAQ supports the PPS Service Rental – Rental Generator SOP. It explains the key steps and decisions in the process, including fleet vs re-rent, Re-Rent POs, freight, check-out/check-in, vendor returns, and closing the rental.

Questions & Answers

1. What is the purpose of the PPS Service Rental SOP?

The PPS Service Rental SOP defines the end-to-end process of renting a generator to a customer. It covers taking the customer’s order, creating the rental contract, deciding whether the generator comes from our own fleet or as a re-rent from a vendor, adding rental/re-rent items, handling freight and billing, checking the unit out and back in, processing any vendor returns for re-rents, and finally closing the rental.

2. Where does this SOP start and end?

The process starts at S1. Customer Places an Order for a Rental Generator and S2. Create Rental Contract.

It ends at S15. Close Rental, after the generator has been checked in and, for re-rent situations, any required vendor return steps have been completed.

3. What is the difference between fleet rentals and re-rents?

The decision “Is this a rental generator in our fleet?” (D1) splits the flow into:

  • Fleet rentals: The generator is supplied from our own rental fleet. You use S4. Add Rental Items to add the unit and any related lines.
  • Re-rents: The generator is supplied by a vendor. You use S3. Add Re Rent Items and then follow the Re-Rent PO steps (S5–S7) to manage the vendor side of the transaction.
4. How are Re-Rent POs handled?

For re-rented generators, the Re-Rent PO flow is:

  • S3. Add Re Rent Items – Add the items being re-rented from a vendor.
  • S5. Raise Re Rent PO – Create the purchase order to the vendor.
  • S6. Print Re Rent PO – Print or otherwise issue the PO.
  • S7. Receive Re Rent PO – Record receipt of the generator into the system.

Only after this flow is complete is the rental ready for billing review, printing the Rental Agreement, and Check Out.

5. When do we add or review freight charges?

Freight is controlled by the decision “Are we Charging freight?” (D2) and handled at S9. Add Freight, after S8. Change/Check Billing Details.

If freight should be charged, you add or confirm it at S9 so the rental billing is accurate. If no freight is charged, you move past S9 without adding freight lines.

6. What is the sequence around billing, the Rental Agreement, and Check Out?

Before the generator leaves the yard, the typical sequence is:

  • S8. Change/Check Billing Details – Review or update billing information.
  • S9. Add Freight – Add freight charges if applicable.
  • S10. Print Rental Agreement – Print the Rental Agreement for the customer.
  • S11. Check Out Rental – Check the generator out to the customer.

This ensures that billing and paperwork are correct at the moment the generator leaves.

7. What happens at Check In for fleet rentals vs re-rents?

At S12. Check In Rental, the generator is returned from the customer:

  • For fleet rentals, Check In records that our own unit has returned, and you move toward closing the rental once billing is complete.
  • For re-rents, the decision “Is this a Re-Rent?” (D3) determines whether a vendor return is needed before closing the rental.
8. What is the role of the Return Merchandise PO?

For re-rented generators, you use a Return Merchandise PO to return the unit to the vendor:

  • S13. Print Return Merchandise PO – Creates and prints the vendor return document.
  • S14. Receive Vendor Return Merchandise PO – Records the vendor receiving the unit back.

This keeps vendor inventory and financials aligned with the actual return of the re-rented generator.

9. When is it appropriate to close the rental?

You should complete S15. Close Rental only when:

  • The generator has been checked in at S12.
  • For re-rents, the vendor return steps (S13–S14) have been completed as needed.
  • Billing details are correct and any outstanding rental activity has been captured.

Closing too early can cause missing charges or incomplete return tracking; closing too late can clutter open rental lists.

10. How does this Rental SOP fit into the larger PPS / Scott Electric process?

The Rental SOP is one piece of the broader PPS / Scott Electric service offering:

  • Upstream: A customer need is identified, and a rental is chosen as the solution (for example, project power, temporary backup, or outage support).
  • This SOP: Manages the rental lifecycle – contract creation, fleet vs re-rent decision, Re-Rent PO if needed, freight and billing details, check-out, check-in, vendor return for re-rents, and close rental.
  • Downstream: Information from this SOP supports accurate billing, P&L reporting for rental business, and decisions on when to buy additional fleet units versus relying on re-rents.

Getting this process right reduces disputes, improves financial accuracy, and improves decisions about fleet investment versus vendor re-rent.
