PPS – Service Start-Up – Generator Start-Up – Frequently Asked Questions

This FAQ supports the PPS Service Start-Up – Generator Start-Up at PPS Service SOP. It explains the key steps and decisions in the process, from the customer’s initial call through unit registration.

Questions & Answers

1. What is the purpose of the PPS Service Start-Up SOP?

The PPS Service Start-Up SOP defines the end-to-end process for handling generator start-up requests at PPS Service. It covers the customer’s initial call, confirming whether PPS sold the generator, locating or creating the unit in the system, verifying that the sale has been paid, creating a start-up incident, scheduling and preparing the job, capturing shipping feedback, performing the start-up, and registering the unit.

2. Where does this SOP start and end?

The process starts at S1. Customer Calls For Start-Up, when a customer contacts PPS Service to request generator start-up.

It ends at S14. Register A Unit, when the unit is registered in the system after S13. Tech Performs Start-Up. Between those points, the SOP ensures PPS has a valid sale, a known unit, and an organized incident and schedule before technicians go to the field.

3. Why is “Did we sell the generator?” (D1) such an important decision?

The decision D1 – Did we sell the generator? determines the overall path:

  • If Yes, PPS can normally proceed with start-up under its standard terms, using S2. Order Inquiry and S3. Check With AR To Verify Payment In Full to confirm details and payment status.
  • If No, PPS may still choose to perform start-up, but must first ensure a proper unit record exists in the system (for example, by using S6. Create A New Unit SOP) and may need to confirm commercial terms before scheduling work.

This avoids performing start-ups with incomplete commercial or unit information.

4. How do we locate the original order or unit in the system?

The SOP uses two main paths, driven by D2 – Do You Know The Original Order #?

  • If the original order number is known, use S2. Order Inquiry to quickly find the order and confirm the details.
  • If the order number is not known, use S5. Order Inquiry Using Serial Number (or similar searches) to identify the original order or at least the unit.

If no prior record exists in the system for a unit that PPS is being asked to start up, S6. Create A New Unit SOP is used to create one.

5. What is the role of AR in this process?

At S3. Check With AR To Verify Payment In Full, AR confirms that the customer has paid (or is appropriately cleared) for the generator and any associated start-up charges. This step:

  • Protects PPS from performing start-ups where commercial obligations are not met.
  • Prevents disputes and rework later.

Only after AR confirmation does the process move on to creating the start-up incident and scheduling work.

6. Why do we create a Start-Up Incident?

In S4. Create Start-Up Incident, the user creates an incident/ticket in the service system. This:

  • Ties the request to the customer, generator, and any related order or unit records.
  • Provides a single reference number that technicians, coordinators, and AR can all see.
  • Forms the basis for subsequent steps such as notes, scheduling, and pick ticket creation.

It is a key organizing object for the entire start-up job.

7. What happens between “Add Start-Up Notes” and “Shipping Feedback Ship”?

The SOP includes a series of steps to prepare and document the start-up job:

  • S8. Add Start-Up Notes – Document key details (site conditions, access, special instructions, safety items).
  • S9. Add Line – Add the necessary line(s) for work or materials on the service order or related document.
  • S10. Schedule – Schedule the start-up visit with an appropriate date/time and technician.
  • S11. Print Pick Ticket – Print any required pick ticket so materials/tools can be staged.
  • S12. Shipping Feedback Ship – Record that materials or equipment have shipped or been confirmed for the job.

This sequence ensures the technician arrives with the right information and equipment.

8. What is captured at “Tech Performs Start-Up” (S13)?

At S13. Tech Performs Start-Up, the technician:

  • Performs the actual start-up in the field (checks, testing, adjustments).
  • Verifies that the unit operates correctly under the expected conditions.
  • Records findings, any issues, and further recommendations in the service documentation.

This step is the core value-add for the customer and is often where future PMA opportunities are identified.

9. Why is unit registration the final step?

At S14. Register A Unit, the generator is formally registered in the system. This:

  • Links the serial number and key technical details to the customer and site.
  • Supports warranty administration and future service history.
  • Helps PPS/Palco understand the installed base and plan future PM or upgrade campaigns.

Without registration, it is harder to track future work and support the customer effectively.

10. How does this Start-Up SOP fit into the broader PPS / Scott Electric process?

The Start-Up SOP is one slice of the overall generator lifecycle:

  • Upstream: A generator is sold and delivered to the customer.
  • This SOP: The customer (or salesperson) requests start-up; PPS verifies sale and payment, organizes the work, performs the start-up, and registers the unit.
  • Downstream: The registered unit and start-up history support future PMA opportunities, warranty work, and service orders throughout the generator’s life.

A disciplined start-up process reduces issues at go-live, builds customer confidence, and sets up future service revenue streams.
