PPS – Service PMA – Preventive Maintenance Agreements – Frequently Asked Questions

This FAQ supports the PPS Service PMA – PMA Contract to Service Order SOP. It explains the key steps and decisions in the process, including contract setup, schedule, fixed billing, the Contract SRO generator, SRO creation, and the hand‑off into the main Service Order SOP.

Questions & Answers

1. What is the purpose of the PPS Service PMA SOP?

The PPS Service PMA SOP defines the end‑to‑end process for managing preventive maintenance agreements (PMAs) for generators. It covers creating the PMA contract from a template, linking it to the correct generator, defining the maintenance schedule, entering the fixed billing total, running the Contract SRO generator, and creating SROs for upcoming PMA visits that then flow into the main Service Order SOP.

2. Where does this SOP start and end?

The process starts at S1. Contract is created using a template and a Line/SN is added and S2. Contract Maintenance schedule is filled out.

It ends at S5. SRO is generated for all required PMA services in the next month and S6. See Service Order Process starting from Schedule, where control passes to the standard Service Order SOP for scheduling and executing the work.

3. Why is it important to create the PMA contract from a template and add the Line/SN?

In S1, the user creates the contract from a standard template and adds the line/serial number of the generator covered by the PMA. This:

  • Ensures the correct PMA structure (billing, terms, and fields) is used.
  • Links the PMA to a specific generator so that future SROs and maintenance activity clearly reference the right unit.
  • Improves traceability for both the customer and PPS/Palco.
4. How is the maintenance schedule determined and recorded?

In S2. Contract Maintenance schedule is filled out, the user records when service is required for the generator. The schedule typically uses the first day of the month for whichever month the service is requested. This:

  • Aligns all PMA services due in a given month to a single reference date.
  • Makes it easy for the Contract SRO generator to pick up all PMAs due in that month.

Any detailed cadence (annual, semi‑annual, quarterly, etc.) is reflected in the schedule pattern chosen on the contract.

5. What is the role of the fixed billing total?

In S3. Fixed billing total is entered, the user records the agreed fixed billing total for the PMA. This amount is used so that:

  • Billing for the PMA can be generated consistently (for example, monthly or annually) without recalculating each time.
  • Finance and sales share a single agreed amount for the contract.

This step improves financial accuracy and avoids surprises later.

6. Why is the Contract SRO generator run in the middle of each month?

In S4. Contract SRO generator utility is run in the middle of each month, the system looks ahead and creates SROs for PMA services due in the next month. Running it mid‑month:

  • Gives planners time to review the upcoming workload.
  • Allows schedulers to contact customers early and coordinate access to the equipment.
  • Helps balance technician loading by seeing next‑month PMA work in advance.
7. What happens when SROs are generated for PMA services?

In S5. SRO is generated for all required PMA services in the next month, the system creates Service Orders (SROs) for each PMA visit due in that upcoming month. These SROs:

  • Represent the actual work that technicians will perform in the field.
  • Form the bridge from PMA contract planning into the day‑to‑day Service Order SOP.

After this point, scheduling, dispatch, execution, and completion are managed using the standard Service Order process.

8. How does the PMA SOP link to the main Service Order SOP?

S6. See Service Order Process starting from Schedule means that once the SROs exist, you follow the standard Service Order SOP starting at the Schedule step. In practice, that means:

  • Using the newly created PMA SROs in the Schedule step.
  • Dispatching technicians, performing the work, capturing labor and materials, and completing the SRO as normal.

The PMA SOP sets up the work; the Service Order SOP executes it.

9. Who typically initiates PMA opportunities?

PMA opportunities are often initiated in two ways:

  • PMA Sales staff proactively contact customers who are good candidates for preventive maintenance agreements.
  • Customers with recently installed generators call in on the suggestion of a technician, who recommends a PMA to protect their investment.

This combination of proactive outreach and technician guidance helps build a healthy PMA base.

10. How does PMA activity support the overall business and the customer?

A well‑run PMA process:

  • Helps customers avoid unplanned outages by keeping generators maintained on a predictable schedule.
  • Provides more stable and predictable workload for technicians.
  • Produces steady, relatively predictable revenue from PMA billing.
  • Feeds accurate data into the Service Order SOP for analysis of PM work volume, profitability, and staffing needs.

In short, the PMA SOP turns one‑time installs into long‑term, proactive service relationships.
